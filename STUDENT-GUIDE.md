# Vector Circle — Student Guide

**Track the courses. Hold the streak. Climb the board.**

Vector Circle is where your circle works through a milestone together. A
milestone is a syllabus — a set of courses, in order, with a deadline. Right now
the milestone is **RAG Specialist**: 22 steps over 8 weeks, every course free.

You mark your own progress. Everyone can see it. That's the whole idea.

---

## Before you start

Your admin gives you two things:

- **Your name** — already in the app, you pick it from a list
- **A PIN** — temporary; you'll choose your own in a minute

There is no signup, no email, no password. If you're not in the list, you're not
enrolled yet — ask your admin.

---

## 1 · Open it

Go to the link your admin shared.

**Put it on your home screen.** It's a proper app — it opens full screen, without
the browser bars, and it's faster.

- **Android / Chrome** — tap **Install** in the top bar, or Chrome's menu → *Add
  to Home screen*
- **iPhone / Safari** — Share button → *Add to Home Screen*

First you'll see the day's quote. It's the same quote for everyone in the circle
that day. Tap to move on.

## 2 · Pick your milestone

Tap the milestone card you're enrolled in — **RAG Specialist**. It shows how many
courses, how many weeks, and how many students are in it.

## 3 · Sign in

Pick your name from the list — start typing to jump to it — and enter your PIN.

Get it wrong 8 times and the account locks for 15 minutes. That's deliberate: a
4-digit PIN is only 10,000 guesses.

## 4 · Choose your own PIN

The first time in, you'll be asked to replace the PIN your admin gave you. You
can't skip it, and you can't reach the app until you do.

Why: your admin knows the PIN they gave you, and so might whoever was standing
nearby. Pick 4–6 digits only you know. Six is much harder to guess than four.
It can't be 1234.

## Track — the important one

### At the top

- **Your percentage** and **rank** in the circle
- **Dots filled** out of the milestone total
- **Courses open** — e.g. *3 of 22*. Your admin opens the syllabus week by week,
  so most of it starts locked. Nothing is broken.
- **Pace** — *ahead*, *on pace*, or *behind*. The white tick on the bar is where
  you should be today, based on the milestone's start and end dates.
- **The reward** — what the first person to finish takes.

### Study streak

Tap **Log today**, enter the minutes you studied, done. The streak counts
consecutive days. Miss a day and it resets to zero.

It shows on the board as 🔥 next to your name once you're past two days. It isn't
part of your rank — it's a separate, honest signal about whether you're actually
showing up.

### The courses

Grouped by week, in order. Each card shows:

- **The type** — course, paper, project, or reference
- **How long it takes**
- **What you'll be able to do afterwards** when you're done. Read this. If you
  can't do the thing, you're not done, whatever the dots say.
- **Open ↗** — takes you to the course. All of them are free to audit.

### Dots — how progress works

**Ten dots per course.** Tap the dot you've reached. Tap dot 6 and you're at
6/10. Tap the dot you're already on to step back one.

There is no "done" button on purpose. A five-hour course isn't done or not-done
for most of a week — it's somewhere in between, and the circle should be able to
see that you're moving.

**A rough guide:** dots are how far *through* it you are. Halfway through the
videos is 5. Finished the videos but haven't touched the code is about 7.
Finished, coded along, and you could explain it to someone else is 10.

Ten dots go green. That course is done.

**Nobody checks.** Dots are yours to set. You can put 10 on everything in a
minute and top the board by lunchtime. You'd also walk into an interview unable
to answer the first question, and everyone would find out then instead of now.
The board is a mirror, not a scoreboard.

### Locked courses

Dashed border, dimmed, an amber **🔒 locked** tag. You can read the title, the
provider and what it teaches — you just can't start it or mark dots yet.

They stay visible so you can see the whole road. Your admin opens them when the
circle gets there.

### Notes

Tap **Notes** on any open course to ask a doubt or leave a tip. It stays attached
to that course, so the next person who hits the same wall finds your question —
and your answer — exactly where they need it.

This is the best thing you can do for your circle. Post the thing that confused
you.

## Board

Ranked on **total dots**. Ties go to whoever got there first.

- **Your row is outlined** so you can find yourself
- 🥇🥈🥉 for the top three
- 🔥 and a number = current study streak
- **The strip under each name** is that person's whole milestone — one bar per
  course, filled to their dots. Faint bars are courses still locked. You can read
  someone's month in half a second.
- When someone clears every dot, they get a banner at the top.

Rank moves as people work. Being behind in week 2 means nothing. Being behind in
week 7 means something.

## Circle

The chat room for everyone in this milestone.

Course-specific doubts belong in **Notes** on the course card — that's where
they'll be useful later. Circle is for everything else: study sessions, links,
someone talking themselves out of quitting on a Wednesday.

If your admin has turned off your posting, you'll still read everything — the
box just tells you so. Ask them.

## Library

Papers, documentation and tools worth reading. Not tracked, not scored, no dots.

Read these when a course leaves you with a question. The RAG survey paper is
worth skimming early and returning to often.

## Your account

Tap the circle with your initial, top right:

- **Change PIN** — any time. You'll need your current one.
- **Sign out**

Next to it, the **sun/moon** button switches between light and dark. It follows
your phone by default and remembers once you choose. Dark for late nights, light
for the library at 3pm.

**Forgot your PIN?** Ask your admin to reset it. You'll get a temporary one and
be asked to choose a new PIN when you sign in. Nobody can recover your old one —
not even the admin.

## The RAG Specialist milestone

Eight weeks, 22 steps, from what an LLM is to a deployed system you can demo.

| Week | What you're doing |
|---|---|
| 1 | Foundations — what LLMs do, prompting, transformers |
| 2 | The RAG core, plus the original paper |
| 3 | Embeddings and vector search |
| 4 | Build it — LangChain, **and ship RAG v1 on your own documents** |
| 5 | Data quality and better retrieval |
| 6 | Evaluation — **put numbers on your own system** |
| 7 | Knowledge graphs, agentic RAG, multimodal |
| 8 | LLMOps, **and deploy a capstone with a written case study** |

The bold ones aren't courses. They're the reason the rest matters.

**Nineteen finished courses and no repository does not make you a specialist,
and a placement panel finds that out in about four minutes.** The checkpoints in
weeks 4, 6 and 8 are what you actually talk about in the interview. Give them the
time they need.

## If something goes wrong

**My name isn't in the list** — you're not enrolled in that milestone. Check
you picked the right one; otherwise ask your admin.

**"That PIN does not match"** — wrong PIN, or wrong milestone. After 8 tries
it locks for 15 minutes.

**"Too many wrong PINs"** — wait it out. It exists to stop someone guessing
their way into your account.

**A dot won't fill** — you'll see a red message saying why. If it says the
database refused it, reload the page; the course may have just been unlocked.

**Everything looks locked** — that's normal at the start. Your admin opens each
week when the circle gets there.

**I'm marked "behind pace" in week 1** — pace compares your dots against the
whole milestone and the time left. Early on, with most courses still locked, this
reads harsh. Watch the trend, not the label.

**The app looks stale or won't update** — pull down to refresh, or close and
reopen it from your home screen.

---

## Three things worth knowing

**The dots are for you.** Nobody audits them. Their only value is that they're
true.

**Post the thing that confused you.** Twenty people studying the same syllabus
alone is twenty people stuck on the same chunk-overlap question. The Notes box is
what makes it a circle.

**Show up more than you catch up.** Forty minutes a day beats six hours on
Sunday, every time. That's what the streak is measuring, and it's the only number
here that predicts whether you'll finish.
